Self-Aligning VLMs

Jan 2024 – Apr 2024 Computer Vision & GenAI

Overview

This project focused on improving the alignment between visual and textual components in Vision-Language Models (VLMs). By leveraging synthetic data and advanced fine-tuning techniques, we aimed to enhance the model's ability to localize objects and understand spatial relationships mentioned in text descriptions.

The Challenge

Traditional VLMs often struggle with precise grounding—the ability to map specific words to the correct regions in an image. This "misalignment" leads to poor performance in complex tasks like zero-shot object detection or high-fidelity image editing where local context is critical.

Solution & Methodology

We developed a pipeline for generating high-quality synthetic datasets where bounding boxes and fine-grained descriptions were perfectly synced. These were used to fine-tune a CLIP-based architecture using Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA). The key innovation was a self-correcting loop that used Stable Diffusion to regenerate edge cases where the alignment score was low.

Results

  • Increased CLIP alignment score by 13% across benchmark datasets.
  • Improved object localization task accuracy by over 10%.
  • Successfully demonstrated more robust zero-shot detection on out-of-distribution images.
