01. About Me

I am a Machine Learning Developer passionate about solving complex problems using state-of-the-art AI. With a Bachelor's in Computer Science and a Master's in Computer Science (ML specialization) from MILA / Université de Montréal, I focus on Representation Learning and Generative Modeling.

Currently a Data Scientist/ML Developer at CRIM since January 2025, my work has primarily focused on synthetic tabular data generation in imbalanced settings, with an emphasis on transformer-based models. I have also contributed to a natural language processing project on the generation of synthetic résumés representative of Québec, leading to the creation of the first publicly available French résumé dataset, as well as to a proof of concept for a graph-based recommendation system.

During my academic training, I participated in numerous projects across a range of domains, including computer vision (medical image segmentation), natural language processing (tweet classification for natural disaster detection), applied data science (expected goals prediction for live NHL games), multimodal learning (improving image–text alignment for vision–language models), and probabilistic graphical models.
